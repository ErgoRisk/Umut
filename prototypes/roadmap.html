1
BambooHR Integration
Ships first
Why first: HR data is the trigger for every downstream action. Shipping this first means every new hire automatically creates clean, structured data in Workwize, which feeds the features that follow. Lowest technical risk, highest immediate value. Quick win that builds momentum.
2
Slack Integration
Ships second
Why second: Now that BambooHR is auto-generating requests, Ilse needs a fast surface to approve and track them. Slack is where she already lives, so adoption is near-immediate. Compounds the BambooHR win by putting the resulting work in the right place.
3
Aggregated Dashboard
Ships third
Why third: Needs 4 to 6 weeks of clean data flowing through BambooHR and Slack before the dashboard is meaningful. Serves Ilse's strategic job rather than her tactical job, natural second phase once daily ops are running smoothly.
4
3PL App
Ships last
Why last: Highest complexity, external users, partner adoption risk. Requires Workwize's internal platform to be solid first so ops can support partners through migration. Bigger bet, bigger payoff, but needs the runway.
The thesis: sequence from internal wins (HR data, daily Slack ops) to strategic visibility (dashboard) to the biggest external bet (partner network). Each feature builds on the one before it, and risk scales up as the platform matures.
